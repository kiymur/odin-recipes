Beef Stew

Description

This recipe is a hearty beef stew originally authored by Holly Nilsson. This stew is perfect for the fall and winter months and is sure to help fight off a cold. This recipe can be served on it's own but also pairs well with bread or biscuits. It is also possible to freeze the stew in advance as meal prep.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs stewing beef
  • 3 tbsp AP flour
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 6 cups beef broth
  • 1/2 cup red wine, optional
  • 1 lb potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 4 carrots, cut into 1 inch pieces
  • 4 ribs celery, cut into 1 inch pieces
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary, or 1 sprig fresh
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch, or as needed
  • 2 tbsp water, or as needed
  • 3/4 cup peas

Steps

  1. Combine flour, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Toss beef in flour mixture.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Shake off excess flour from the beef and brown it in small batches, then set aside in a bowl.
  3. Add the onions to the pot, adding more oil as needed, and cool until they begin to soften, about 3 minutes.
  4. Add beef broth and red wine while scrapping up any brown bits in the pan.
  5. Stir in browned beef, potatoes, carrots, celery, tomato paste, and rosemary. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer 1 hour or until beef is tender (up to 90 minutes).
  6. Mix equal parts cornstarch and water and create a slurry. Slowly add the slurry to the boiling stew to reach desired consistency (you may not need all of the slurry, if you'd like a thicker stew, you can add extra).
  7. Stir in peas and simmer 5-10 minutes before serving. Season with salt & pepper to taste.
